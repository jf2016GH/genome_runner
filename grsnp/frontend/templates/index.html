GenomeRunner WEB: Epigenomic enrichment and similarity analyses of sets of SNPs

GenomeRunner WEB is a tool for automating functional interpretation of sets of SNPs by considering their co-localization with functional/regulatory genome annotation data (regulatory elements). GenomeRunner WEB is agnostic to the location of protein-coding regions, utilizing whole-genome nature of genome annotation data. Instead of focusing on common SNPs, GenomeRunner WEB permits the analysis of rare variants and SNPs in non-coding regions by assessing their collective effect upon functional/regulatory landscape.

As an output, GenomeRunner WEB calculates enrichment p-values for each set of SNPs - regulatory element, visualizes the results as a heatmap, and produces sortable tables with p-values. For two or more sets of SNPs, GenomeRunner WEB performs 'epigenomic similarity' analysis, and plots a heatmap of sets of SNPs clustered by their 'epigenomic connectivity'.
